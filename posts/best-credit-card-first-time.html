Best First Credit Card: A Brutal Checklist That Actually Works

Dec 29, 2025
By: Riya Mehta

Picking your first credit card shouldn’t feel like decoding a secret contract. Here’s a simple, brutal checklist you can use in 10 minutes.

Quick idea

If the card’s benefits are confusing, it’s usually because the fees are doing the talking.

The 5-point checklist

  1. Annual fee: Start with zero unless the value is obvious.
  2. Reward fit: Choose rewards based on what you spend on every week.
  3. Interest rate: If you might carry a balance, prioritize APR.
  4. Acceptance: Make sure it actually works where you live and spend.
  5. Customer support: You’ll care about this the first time something breaks.

What “good rewards” really mean

Rewards are only “good” when they survive your habits. A 5% category reward is worthless if you forget to activate it.

Brutal truth: the best card is the one you’ll use consistently without mental overhead.

A mini comparison table

Card TypeBest ForRisk
CashbackSimple, predictable valueLower upside

Common traps (and how to dodge them)

  • “Welcome bonus” tunnel vision: don’t overspend to chase points.
  • Hidden categories: check caps, exclusions, and redemption rules.
  • Fee stacking: foreign transaction fees add up fast.

Example: a safe starter setup

Use a zero-fee cashback card, set autopay for the full statement balance, and track spending weekly.

// Mini rule of thumb
if (you_pay_full_balance_every_month) pick("cashback");
else pick("low APR");
